# Tips for Better Timesheet Image Quality

To improve OCR extraction accuracy, follow these guidelines when capturing timesheet images:

## 📸 Photography Tips

### Lighting
- ✅ **Use bright, even lighting** - Natural daylight or bright indoor lighting works best
- ✅ **Avoid shadows** - Don't block the light source with your hand/phone
- ❌ **Avoid glare** - Don't use flash directly, it can wash out text
- ❌ **Avoid backlighting** - Don't photograph against a window

### Camera Position
- ✅ **Hold phone directly above** - Keep camera parallel to the timesheet
- ✅ **Fill the frame** - Get close enough that the timesheet fills most of the image
- ✅ **Keep it steady** - Use both hands or rest phone on a surface
- ❌ **Avoid angles** - Don't shoot from the side (causes distortion)
- ❌ **Avoid blur** - Make sure image is in focus before capturing

### Background
- ✅ **Use dark/contrasting background** - Makes white timesheet stand out
- ✅ **Flat surface** - Place timesheet on a desk or table
- ❌ **Avoid busy backgrounds** - Remove clutter from around the timesheet

## 📱 Image Quality Settings

### Phone Camera Settings
- Use the **highest resolution** available
- Enable **HDR mode** if available (helps with contrast)
- Disable **beauty mode** or filters
- Use the **standard photo mode** (not panorama or other special modes)

### After Capture
- **Check the image** - Zoom in to verify all numbers are readable
- **Retake if needed** - It's worth getting a good photo the first time
- **Don't crop too aggressively** - Keep some margin around the timesheet

## 🔍 Specific Challenges with Handwritten Timesheets

### Ink Color Issues
- **Red/Pink ink** is harder to read than black ink
  - Solution: Ensure extra bright lighting for red ink
  - Consider using a scanning app with contrast enhancement
  
### Common Number Confusion
The AI may confuse these handwritten numbers:
- **0 vs 6** - Make sure circles are clearly closed or open
- **1 vs 7** - Ensure clear distinction in stroke style
- **3 vs 8** - Look for clear separation in the "3"
- **5 vs 6** - Verify the loop direction

### Time Format
- Typical work times are **6:00 AM - 8:00 PM** (06:00 - 20:00 in 24-hour format)
- If OCR extracts something like "30:45", it's likely misread
- Review entries that fall outside normal work hours

## 🎯 Before vs After Example

### ❌ Poor Quality Image
- Taken at an angle
- Shadows across the page
- Blurry numbers
- Low contrast
- **Result**: OCR fails or extracts incorrect data

### ✅ Good Quality Image
- Taken directly overhead
- Even, bright lighting
- Sharp focus on all numbers
- High contrast between paper and ink
- **Result**: OCR successfully extracts 95%+ of data correctly

## 🔄 Testing Your Images

Before uploading to the app:
1. **Zoom in** on the image on your phone
2. **Verify you can read** every handwritten number
3. **Check for glare** or shadows obscuring text
4. **Ensure straight alignment** - no tilting or skewing
5. If you can't read it easily, **the OCR won't either** - retake!

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Flatten the timesheet** - Use books to weigh down corners if curled
2. **Clean the surface** - Dust or coffee stains can confuse OCR
3. **Batch process** - Take photos of all timesheets at once in good lighting
4. **Name files clearly** - Use "Name_Front.jpg" and "Name_Back.jpg" convention
5. **Keep originals** - Don't delete photos until you've verified the extraction

## 🛠️ Image Preprocessing (Advanced)

If you have photo editing skills, you can improve images before uploading:

1. **Increase contrast** - Makes text stand out from background
2. **Adjust brightness** - Ensure paper is white, not gray
3. **Sharpen** - Makes blurry text more readable (don't overdo it)
4. **Convert to grayscale** - Sometimes helps OCR by removing color distractions
5. **Crop tightly** - Remove unnecessary borders

### Using built-in photo editor:
- **iOS**: Photos app → Edit → Adjust brightness and contrast
- **Android**: Photos app → Edit → Adjust light and color
